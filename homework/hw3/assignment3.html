# Data analysis and cleaning:
# Remove the "county" column
# The "district" and "school" are ID attributes which are not useful for regression analysis,
# they are removed from the data and will not be use in the model.
# Two columns are character type, "computer" & "income", convert to numeric.
# The "grades" column is also character type, convert to factor for analysis.
data_1a = read.csv("CASchools.csv") |>
  select(-county, -district, -school) |>
  mutate(
    across(
      c(students, computer, income),
      ~ suppressWarnings(as.numeric(.x))
    ),
    grades = factor(grades)
  )

Discussion (Question 1a).

  1. Predictors vs math. The full regression model shows strong negative association with lunch (Percent qualifying for reduced-price lunch), and a smaller positive association with income and math both clearly displayed in the plot matrix. The expenditure, students, teachers, and calworks are weaker in this fit after adjusting for the others.

  2. Insignificant values (p > 0.05). I used a filter to sort parameters where p > 0.05, this means the parameter is insignificant and will not contribute anything meaningful to the model. Store these values inside a variable named insignificant_vars. The following variables have p-values above 0.05: students, teachers, calworks, expenditure; also computer is marginal though still above the threshold.

  3. Strongest predictor. By evidence in the data, it is clear that lunch has the largest |t| and smallest p-value. among the predictors making it the strongest linear signal in this model. This is found by scaling all the parameters in the data set.

  4. lunch coefficient. With other predictors held fixed, each one percentage-point increase in lunch is associated with an estimated decrease of about 0.37 points in average math test score. In other words, areas with higher percent of students qualifying for reduced-price lunch, our model predicts a significantly larger portion of students with lower average math scores than areas with lower percentage of students on reduced-prices lunch.
